Why this matters

Fewer than 200 fluent speakers of Secwepemctsín remain today, and most of them are Elders.

This is healing work, and it cannot wait.

For generations, Secwépemc language and knowledge lived in voices, stories, songs, and ceremony. Colonial policies, including the residential school system, broke that chain of passing down within living memory.

Today, young people want to learn, but rich and authentic access is scarce, and time is short. HorAIzon exists to put the voices, art, and teachings of Elders into the hands of the next generation, kept under the community's own control at every step.

What we are building

One platform, three connected experiences.

A place to learn the language, to witness the art, and to safeguard the voices. The three are woven together, and made beautiful enough that people genuinely want to spend time inside it.

Priority · first

The Learner App

An audio-first learning path made for youth. It feels like the way you would learn French, but it is built around the real voices of Elders.

  • Read-along, where each word lights up as you hear an Elder speak it
  • Structured courses, modules, and lessons that give a clear path
  • Daily practice, pronunciation, and gentle progress tracking
  • Animated and playful, while staying respectful and never childish
Priority · second

The Art Gallery

A cinematic gallery of Secwépemc paintings and visual works, where every piece can tell its own story aloud.

  • High-resolution works you can lean into and explore closely
  • Rich context and meaning, with the artist always credited
  • Spoken narration, offered in both Secwepemctsín and English
  • Curated collections and changing exhibits
Priority · third

The Cultural Archive

A secure and lasting home for the recordings, oral histories, songs, and photographs held by the community.

  • Search across recordings, stories, and transcripts
  • Browse by speaker, theme, dialect, and era
  • Community-set access levels: public, community, and restricted
  • Preserved properly, so it survives for future generations
AI-generated · pending review A quiet, careful helper

AI assists the work. It never speaks for the community.

HorAIzon uses leading multimodal AI, currently Google's Gemini, to do the heavy lifting. It turns recordings into text, aligns audio to written words, and drafts stories and lessons. In all of this, the AI is a helper, never an author.

Everything the AI produces is clearly labeled, grounded in approved material, and held back until a person, and ultimately an Elder, reviews and approves it.

Reviewed before anyone sees itNothing the AI makes is published as authentic until a person approves it.
Grounded, not inventedIt works only from approved sources, so it cannot make things up.
Sacred and restricted stays privateProtected knowledge is never sent to any outside service.
How I will get it done

Safeguards first. Then the experience.

The order is deliberate. We build trust and protection into the foundation before a single piece of cultural content goes in, and only then do we layer the experiences on top.

Build the foundation

Establish the look and feel, and the secure, respectful groundwork that the whole platform stands on.

Protect before we populate

Ownership, consent, access levels, and Elder review are all built and working before any real content is added.

Start with learning

Deliver the Learner App first, with read-along, courses, and practice. This is the experience youth will reach for every day.

Add the Gallery

Bring in the Art Gallery, with visual works presented alongside their stories and spoken narration.

Add the Archive

Stand up the searchable Cultural Archive of recordings and oral histories from Elders.

Open and review

Prepare the platform for many Nations, and bring a polished version to the community for guidance and consent.

While we build, we use openly available and placeholder material only. We never use unconsented cultural content. The platform opens to the public only after an Elder partner has reviewed it and given their blessing.

Built on respect

The community holds the pen.

These are not add-ons. They are the foundation, and they follow recognized Indigenous data sovereignty principles, built directly into how the platform works.

The community owns everything

All language, stories, and cultural material belong to the community, not to the platform or the developers. They can take it with them at any time.

Ownership, Control, Access, Possession

Consent, and the right to change it

Nothing is shared without informed agreement, and that consent can be withdrawn at any time. When it is withdrawn, the content comes down.

Free, Prior, and Informed Consent

Authentic voices first

Real Elder voices and real artwork are the heart of HorAIzon. AI stays a quiet helper working in the background.

People over machines

Safe by default

New material stays private until it has been reviewed. Sacred and restricted knowledge is protected and never exposed to outside services.

Private until approved

Built for many Nations

Each community's language, dialects, and identity are kept distinct, so the platform can grow across Canada and beyond without losing what makes each one unique.

One platform, many voices

Made to last

This is built to remain useful for decades. It is open, maintainable, and able to be run and governed by the community itself over time.

Generations, not quarters
